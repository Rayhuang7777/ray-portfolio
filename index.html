About

Building stages
for youth voices

I'm Ray Huang, a high school student based in Chongqing who uses music, photography, and public engagement to create spaces where young people can find and use their voices.

As the lead vocalist and guitarist of my band, music was my first mode of expression. That energy extended into organising: student media, cultural events, Model United Nations, and bringing the Floating Music Festival to Chongqing.

I see myself not just as a performer or observer, but as someone who builds the stage others perform on — someone who asks what structures allow creativity and civic life to thrive.

Central inquiry

How can culture, creative industries, and public spaces strengthen youth participation and social connection?

This question sits at the intersection of politics, economics, sociology, and culture. I want to understand how music festivals, youth communities, and civic spaces shape a city's life — and how young people move from participants to architects of that conversation.
